<p><p>Pervious pavement refers to a type of surface that allows water to pass through, reducing runoff and preventing flooding. The market includes three main types: pervious concrete, porous asphalt, and interlocking concrete pavers. Pervious concrete is made from a mix of cement and coarse aggregates, allowing water to infiltrate through the pavement. Porous asphalt has small holes that allow water to seep through. Interlocking concrete pavers have open joints that permit water drainage. These solutions help manage stormwater effectively.</p></p>
