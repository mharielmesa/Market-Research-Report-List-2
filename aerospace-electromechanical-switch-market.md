<p><ul><li>Tactile</li><li>Rotary</li><li>Encoder</li><li>Toggle</li><li>Push</li><li>Detect</li><li>Micro</li><li>Dip</li><li>Other (Power, Slide, Joy Stick, etc.)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Aerospace Electromechanical Switch market offers a variety of types to cater to different switching needs. Tactile switches provide a physical feedback upon activation, while Rotary switches can be turned to select different options. Encoder switches convert rotary movement into digital signals. Toggle switches have a lever that moves up and down to toggle between on/off positions. Push switches are activated by pressing down on them. Detect switches are triggered by external stimuli. Micro switches are small and sensitive. Dip switches are used for setting binary configurations. Other types include Power switches, Slide switches, and Joy Stick switches, each serving specific functions in aerospace applications.</p></p>
