<p><p>Textural food ingredients are categorized into three main types: hydrocolloids, functional proteins, and starches. Hydrocolloids are gelling and thickening agents sourced from plants, seaweeds, and microorganisms. Functional proteins, such as whey and soy proteins, provide texture, emulsification, and moisture retention properties to food products. Starches, derived from corn, potatoes, and grains, are widely used as thickeners and stabilizers in various food applications. These ingredients play a crucial role in enhancing the texture, mouthfeel, and overall quality of food products.</p></p>
