<p><p>High performance alloys can be classified into four main types based on their specific properties and applications. Superalloys are designed to withstand high temperatures and extreme environments, making them ideal for use in aerospace and power generation industries. Corrosion-resistant alloys are specifically formulated to resist degradation from chemical exposure, often used in chemical processing and oil & gas industries. Electronic alloys are tailored for use in electronic devices and components, providing high conductivity and stability. Wear-resistant alloys are engineered to withstand wear and abrasion, commonly employed in mining and manufacturing applications.</p></p>
