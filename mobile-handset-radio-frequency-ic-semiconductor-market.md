<p><p>N-Type Semiconductors are materials that have an excess of electrons, allowing them to conduct electricity more easily. They are commonly used in the mobile handset radio frequency IC semiconductor market for their fast operation and low resistance. On the other hand, P-Type Semiconductors have an excess of positively charged "holes" which move in the opposite direction to electrons. These semiconductors are also important in the market as they complement N-type semiconductors, allowing for the creation of diodes and transistors necessary for RF ICs in mobile handsets.</p></p>
