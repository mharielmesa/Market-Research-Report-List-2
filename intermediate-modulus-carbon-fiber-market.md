<p><p>Intermediate modulus carbon fiber is classified based on the number of filaments per tow, with common types being 1K, 3K, 6K, 12K, and above 12K. The number represents the quantity of individual carbon filaments bundled together to form a tow. The higher the number, the larger the bundle size and thicker the carbon fiber strand. Each type has its own unique properties and is used in various applications, depending on specific strength, stiffness, and weight requirements.</p></p>
