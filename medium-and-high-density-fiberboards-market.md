<p><p>Medium Density Fiberboards (MDF) are a type of engineered wood product made by breaking down hardwood or softwood residuals into fibers and combining them with wax and a resin binder. MDF is known for its smooth surface and uniform density, making it ideal for furniture, cabinetry, and construction applications. On the other hand, High Density Fiberboards (HDF) are similar to MDF but with a higher density and greater strength, making them suitable for heavy-duty applications such as flooring and wall panels. Both MDF and HDF offer cost-effective and versatile alternatives to natural wood products in various industries.</p></p>
