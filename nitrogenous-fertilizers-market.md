<p><p>Nitrogenous fertilizers are essential for promoting plant growth and increasing crop yields. The four main types of nitrogenous fertilizers are urea, ammonium nitrate, ammonium sulfate, and calcium ammonium nitrate (CAN). Urea is a highly concentrated form of nitrogen that is widely used in agriculture. Ammonium nitrate is known for its fast-acting properties, making it ideal for crops that require immediate nitrogen uptake. Ammonium sulfate is a versatile fertilizer that also provides sulfur to plants. Calcium ammonium nitrate (CAN) is a balanced fertilizer containing both nitrate and ammonium nitrogen, as well as calcium.</p></p>
