<p><p>Titanium Dioxide (CAS 13463-67-7) is commonly produced using two main processes: the Sulfate Process and the Chloride Process. The Sulfate Process involves chemically reacting titanium ore with sulfuric acid, while the Chloride Process involves using chlorine to treat the titanium ore. The Sulfate Process is more commonly used and accounts for a larger share of the market due to its lower production costs. The Chloride Process is typically preferred for higher quality applications due to its ability to produce purer titanium dioxide.</p></p>
