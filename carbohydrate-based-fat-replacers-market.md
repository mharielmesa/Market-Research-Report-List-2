<p><p>Carbohydrate-based fat replacers are used in food products to mimic the texture and mouthfeel of fats while reducing calorie content. Starch fat replacers, such as modified food starch, absorb water and create a creamy texture. Fibrous fat replacers, like cellulose, add bulk and moisture to products. Colloidal fat replacers, such as gums and pectin, stabilize emulsions and provide a smooth texture. Other types of fat replacers include protein-based and lipid-based alternatives, each offering unique benefits for reducing fat content in a variety of food products.</p></p>
