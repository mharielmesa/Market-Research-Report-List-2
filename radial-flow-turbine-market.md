<p><ul><li>Single-stage</li><li>Multistage</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Radial flow turbines are classified based on the number of stages they possess. Single-stage radial flow turbines have only one set of rotor blades, while multistage radial flow turbines have multiple sets of rotor blades arranged sequentially. Single-stage turbines are typically used in applications where lower efficiency and cost are acceptable, while multistage turbines are preferred for high-efficiency and high-pressure applications. The choice between single-stage and multistage turbines depends on factors such as the desired power output, pressure requirements, and cost considerations.</p></p>
