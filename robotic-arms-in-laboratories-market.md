<p><p>Robotic arms in laboratories are typically categorized into four main types: articulated arm, dual arm, parallel link arm, and others. Articulated arms consist of multiple joints, allowing for a wide range of motion and flexibility. Dual arms function similarly to human arms, with separate manipulators for increased dexterity. Parallel link arms use parallel links and joints for precise and stable movement. The "others" category includes unique designs that may combine aspects of the aforementioned types or offer specialized features to suit specific laboratory applications.</p></p>
